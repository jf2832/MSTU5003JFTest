"The internet and digital media have become ubiquitous in our lives. As we become increasingly active online, having a strong understanding of how to be safe, critical, and responsible digital citizens is vital." Design of this Lesson

This lesson is designed to be partly teacher-led but mostly student-led. Using "Student View," students will first be introduced to the topic of internet safety through a synchronous teacher-led slide presentation that students can access through the main banner. Teachers will then review the learning objectives of this lesson and once again students can follow along through their "student view." Students will then be asked to read an informative paragraph about how to stay safe online and then will be asked to review three separate resources that will help them learn more about specific issues relating to internet safety. This portion can be done in class or during the student's own time. Once students finish viewing the resources they will be asked to complete two activities which will act as formative assessments. These two activities are built directly into the "student view." Finally students will complete a final project to demonstrate their knowledge. They will post the link to their finished URL on the "student view" page. This lesson can be completed over a series of days, it can be used to enhance other lessons that the teacher may give in class, or it can be done completely asynchronously if teachers record their initial "lecture" with the provided slides.

Lesson Plan

Grade Level

Grades 6-9

Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:
  • Identify main issues and vocabulary regarding internet safety in the 21st century
  • Analyze the components of a strong password and apply strategies for password creation.
  • Discern between appropriate and inappropriate information to share online.

Procedures & How to Use Student View

  1. Students will be introduced to the topic of internet safety by the instructor using the “Introduction to Internet Safety for Tweens Slides."(Student View: Staying Safe Online - Intro to Internet Safety button)
  2. Students will read over their learning objectives (Student View: Learning Objectives) and the intro to how to stay safe online (Student View: How to Stay Safe Online)
  3. Students will review internet safety vocabulary using Quizlet. Students will play the vocabulary “Matching Game.” The student with the fastest score will get a extra credit on the final assessment. (Student View: View These Resources: Internet Safety Vocabulary Quizlet)
  4. Students will read and watch videos about key internet safety issues by exploring the tutorials on Internet Safety by GCF (Goodwill Community Foundation). They will predominately focus on the issue of creating strong passwords.(Student View: View These Resources: Creating Strong Passwords)
  5. Students will watch the video: “Perspectives on Chatting Safely Online” and will focus on identifying safe and unsafe online behaviors. (Student View: View These Resources: Staying Safe on Social Media)
  6. Students will complete Activity 1: To Post or not to Post

Instructional Tools

  • NetSmartz Workshop: Internet Safety for Tweens Presentation Slides
  • Quizlet: Internet Safety Vocabulary Terms
  • Goodwill Community Foundation LearnFree.org: Internet Safety Tutorials
  • Common Sense Media: Perspectives on Chatting Safely Online
  • Piktochart: Infographic Creator

Assessments

Formative Assessments:
  • Quizlet vocabulary game scores.
  • Activity 1: To Post or not to Post?
  • Activity 2: Password Security
Summative Assessment:

Students will use Piktochart.com and create an infographic that demonstrates what they have learned about a particular issue within internet safety (Ex. Password protection, Security Settings, Malware, etc...).

Infographic Requirements:

  • Introduction to the issue.
  • Include at least 3 pieces of statistical data or facts.
  • List the specific safety concerns/threats.
  • List safe behaviors/what the user can do.
  • Conclusion: How it will impact how they behave online? or What lessons did they learn?
